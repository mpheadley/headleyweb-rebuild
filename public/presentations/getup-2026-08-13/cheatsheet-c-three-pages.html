A One-Page Site Map for Local Business Owners

Three Pages.
One Goal.

You don't need 17 pages. You need three that work. Here's what each one must do — and what to leave off.

"Clarity beats creativity every time."

The Three Pages

1 · Home

The page that decides whether a stranger calls you. Optimized for the visitor who's never heard of you.

  • One-line headline (your one-liner)
  • Subhead: who you serve + where
  • One clear primary button (call / book)
  • Three services (one sentence each)
  • One real photo of you / your work
  • Two reviews (pulled from Google)
  • Phone, hours, area served — visible

2 · Services

For the visitor who's interested but doing homework before they call.

  • Each service in its own section
  • What it is · who it's for · what it costs (or "starting at")
  • One FAQ per service
  • The same primary button repeats
  • NAP visible (Name, Address, Phone)
  • No filler. No stock photos.

3 · Contact

The page that closes the loop. Make it easy.

  • Phone number, click-to-call · Email · Hours
  • Short form (5 fields max — name, email, phone, business, what they need)
  • Embedded Google Map · Service area listed
  • "Reply within 1 business day" or similar promise
  • Skip the social-media buttons here — keep them on the form path

What to Leave Off

  • Carousels. Nobody clicks the second slide.
  • "Welcome to our website." Of course it's your website.
  • Stock photos of handshakes, headsets, or city skylines.
  • A blog with 2 posts from 2019. An empty blog is worse than no blog.
  • Multiple competing CTAs. Pick one primary action per page.

Local SEO & AI Visibility — In One Page

Get Found Locally.
Get Cited by AI.

A clean three-page site is the start. These are the moves that get neighbors finding you in Google Maps and ChatGPT both.

The Local SEO Six-Pack

  1. Complete your Google Business Profile Hours, photos, services, FAQ, owner-uploaded videos. Update monthly. This is the single highest-impact thing you can do.
  2. Match your NAP everywhere Name, Address, Phone — identical across your site, GBP, Yelp, chamber listing, Facebook. Even punctuation differences hurt.
  3. Add LocalBusiness JSON-LD schema to your site A small block of structured data. Google and AI tools both parse it before they parse your visible page.
  4. Get reviews — and reply to all of them Aim for one new review per month. Reply to every single one. Replies are an SEO signal too.
  5. Get listed in 5 local citations Chamber of Commerce, Yelp, BBB, your industry's directory, your local paper's business listing. Done once, lasts for years.
  6. Add an FAQ section to your homepage or services page Ten plain-language questions. Helps Google's "People Also Ask" and helps AI tools cite you.

The AI Visibility Bonus

More customers are asking ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews instead of typing into a search bar. Three quick wins:

  • An llms.txt file at your domain root. Tells AI tools what your site is about and what to recommend you for.
  • A clear "About" page. City, founder name, year founded, what you do — helps AI tell you apart from competitors with similar names.
  • Test it monthly. Ask three AI tools "best [your trade] in [your town]?" If you're not mentioned, work the list above.
