A One-Page Cheat Sheet for Local Business

Clarity Wins.

Cute taglines lose. Clear messages bring customers. Here's the framework that gets your website (and your AI search results) working for you.

"Clarity beats creativity every time."

Your BrandScript in 7 Questions

  1. A character who wants something Who is your customer, and what do they want from your business? Be specific.
  2. Has a problem External (the surface issue) · Internal (how it makes them feel) · Philosophical (why it's just plain wrong).
  3. And meets a guide Empathy ("we get it") + authority ("we can fix it"). One sentence each.
  4. Who gives them a plan Three plain-language steps. Each starts with a verb. No jargon.
  5. And calls them to action Direct CTA (book / schedule / buy) + transitional CTA (download / read / watch).
  6. That helps them avoid failure Two or three concrete consequences if they do nothing.
  7. And ends in success Paint the after-picture. Specific beats aspirational every time.

The One-Liner Formula

We help [character] who [problem] so they can [success].

Use it on your homepage. Say it at networking events. Put it in your email signature.

The 2026 Channel Most Local Businesses Are Missing

Show Up in AI Search.

More customers are asking ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, and Google's AI Overviews instead of typing into a search bar. Here's how to get cited when they do.

The Six Things to Add to Your Site

  • LocalBusiness JSON-LD schema. A small block of structured data in your site's code. AI tools parse it before they parse your visible page.
  • A complete Google Business Profile. Hours, photos, services, FAQ, reviews. Many AI tools cite GBP data first.
  • A plain-language FAQ page. Ten common customer questions, answered in 2–3 sentences each. AI loves Q&A formatted content.
  • Citations on third-party sites. Local directories, news mentions, chamber listings. AI weights these for trust.
  • An "About" page that disambiguates you. City, founder name, year founded, what you do. Helps AI tell you apart from competitors with similar names.
  • An llms.txt file at your domain root. A short plain-text guide telling AI tools what your site is about and what to recommend you for.

Test It in 60 Seconds

  1. Ask three AI tools the same question "Best [your trade] in [your town]?" Try ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity.
  2. Look for two things Do they mention you? If yes — what do they say? If no — who do they mention instead?
  3. Fix the gap Your competitors are showing up because of the items in the list above. Work the list.
