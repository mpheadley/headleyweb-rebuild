A One-Page Prompt Sheet for Local Business

AI as Your Web Assistant.

AI is a fast first-draft writer and a terrible final-draft writer. Use these prompts to get to a draft in minutes — then rewrite it so it sounds like you.

"Clarity beats creativity every time."

Six Prompts You Can Use Today

1 · Homepage hero copy

I run a [type of business] in [city]. My customers are [who]. Their main problem is [problem]. Write 5 homepage hero options: each is one short headline + one sentence of subtext. Plain language. No exclamation marks. No "transform your life."

2 · About-page draft

Write a 200-word About page for [business name] in [city]. Background: [your story in 3 bullets]. Tone: warm, plain, neighborly. End with one specific reason a customer should choose us.

3 · Services page outline

My services are: [list]. For each, write a 60-word block: what it is, who it's for, what it costs (if I told you), and what to do next. Don't add services I didn't list.

4 · FAQ page

Generate 10 FAQs a customer of [type of business] in [city] would actually ask before booking. Answer each in 2–3 sentences using plain language. No corporate phrasing.

5 · Customer research

Pretend you're a [customer type] in [city] looking to hire a [type of business]. List the 8 questions you'd want answered before you'd pick one. Order from most important to least.

6 · Business one-pager

Help me draft a one-page business summary for [business] in [city]: who we serve, what we do, what makes us different, our 3 main services, and a 12-month plan with 3 priorities. Keep each section to 4 sentences.

How to Work With AI Without Sounding Like AI

You're the Editor.

AI can write fast. It can't sound like you. The writers who win are the ones who use AI for speed and themselves for voice.

The Five Rules

  1. Always rewrite the AI's output in your own voice Read it aloud. If it doesn't sound like something you'd say, change it. Even a 30% rewrite makes it yours.
  2. Be specific in your prompt or be disappointed in the output "Write me a homepage" produces beige. "Write a homepage for a barbershop in downtown Anniston serving working-class men 25–55" produces something usable.
  3. Tell it what NOT to do "Don't use exclamation marks. Don't use the words 'transform' or 'unleash.' Don't make up services I didn't list." AI obeys negative prompts well.
  4. Ask for variations, not finals "Give me 5 versions" is almost always better than "give me one." Pick the closest, then iterate.
  5. Never paste customer data or anything confidential Treat AI like a smart freelancer you don't fully trust yet. Same rules apply.

Words to Strike on Sight

Buzzy verbs

  • transform
  • unleash
  • elevate
  • empower
  • revolutionize

Beige adjectives

  • seamless
  • cutting-edge
  • world-class
  • innovative
  • holistic

Clichés

  • "next level"
  • "go-to choice"
  • "trusted partner"
  • "in today's world"
  • "stand out from the crowd"
If a real person wouldn't say it
at a kitchen table, cut it.
