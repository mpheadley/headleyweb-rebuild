# Pricing Decisions Log (March 2026)

Full log of pricing structure decisions, considerations explored, and positions rejected — for continuity across conversations.

## Final Decisions (as of March 2026)

### Build Tiers (live on headleyweb.com)
- **Get Found — $495** — Entry product, simplified scope (1-2 pages, GBP setup, no blog). For side hustles and micro-businesses. Not a speed tier — genuinely different scope.
- **Get Calls — $1,495** — Full marketing site (3-5 pages, blog + 2 launch posts, FAQ schema, GBP setup).
- **Get Booked — $1,995** — Local authority build (5-7 pages, service area/trade pages, 4 launch posts for AEO, full GA4, comprehensive schema, Search Console submitted).

### Care Plans (live)
- **Essential Care — $49/mo** — Hosting, security, 30 min updates/month, uptime monitoring
- **Growth Care — $99/mo** — Everything in Essential + GBP managed, monthly report, review coaching
- **Accelerate — $199/mo** — Everything in Growth + 2 blog posts/month, monthly strategy call, AI visibility monitoring

### Pricing Psychology
- "Best Value" badge on middle tier of BOTH build cards AND care plan cards (compromise effect)
- Top tier = price anchor, not recommendation
- Full target prices shown on site. Founding client callout explains discount is available on inquiry — NOT strike-through pricing.

### Founding Client Strategy
- Prices are value anchors. Founding clients get deeply discounted or free builds in exchange for testimonials and case studies.
- Discounts ease down progressively (free -> heavily discounted -> moderately -> slightly -> full). Never a hard flip to full price. Never announce "prices going up."
- Keep discount offers OFF the site — personal outreach and discovery calls only. The site callout just says "founding client rate available, ask about availability."

### Blogs in Build Tiers
- Get Found: no blog
- Get Calls: blog section built + 2 launch posts (one-time build deliverable)
- Get Booked: blog section built + 4 launch posts
- Ongoing monthly posts = Accelerate care plan only ($199/mo). No cannibalizing.
- Launch posts seed indexable content at launch and make the Accelerate upsell natural.

### Two Buyer Types
- **Side hustles / micro-businesses** -> Get Found ($495). Low urgency, lower budget, welcome but not prioritized.
- **Local service businesses** -> Get Calls / Get Booked. Primary target. Motivated, care plan upsell candidates.
- Matt will take any client but prioritizes real businesses when he has the choice.

## Decisions Explored But Rejected (with reasons)

### Speed-tier model (Lee Blue)
- **What it is:** Every client gets the same complete stack. Tiers differ only by delivery speed (6mo / 90 days / 30 days).
- **Why rejected for Phase 1:** Speed premium doesn't hold at $495-$1,995 price points. Requires knowing actual delivery pace (not established yet). Also requires the full stack (social, email, review systems) that Matt doesn't offer yet.
- **Phase 3 direction:** Revisit once 10+ clients, proven recurring revenue, $500-$1,200/mo retainer credibility.

### Bundled care + build pricing (Lee Blue model)
- **What it is:** Build cost amortized into annual retainer. One price, one decision.
- **Why rejected for Phase 1:** No recurring revenue base yet to know what a bundle is worth. Changes contract structure significantly. Park for Phase 2.

### $2,995 Get Booked
- **Why rejected:** Service area pages + full GA4 + comprehensive schema is ~30-40% more work than Get Calls. $1,995 is more honest. $2,995 is the Phase 2 target once a case study proves what service area pages produce in search.

### $495/$995/$1,995 (doubling pattern)
- **Why explored:** Mirrors care plan doubling ($49/$99/$199), creates cleaner anchor math.
- **Why rejected:** $1,995 top tier too close to $1,495 middle to anchor well. $995 middle vs $495 bottom has a cleaner gap. Ultimately the scope question mattered more than the math.

### $495/$995/$1,495 (compromise pattern)
- **Why explored:** Cleaner numbers, even $500 steps.
- **Why rejected:** $1,495 top doesn't anchor strongly enough — gap from middle isn't wide enough to make Get Calls feel like an obvious win.

### "Most Popular" vs "Best Value" badge on care plans
- **Resolved:** "Best Value" on middle tier (Growth Care, $99) — consistent with build tier badge. Top tier (Accelerate) is price anchor, no badge.

### Stack as differentiator (HTML vs Next.js)
- **Why rejected:** Clients don't understand or care about stack. Matt is using Next.js for essentially all builds now. Not a meaningful client-facing differentiator.

### Brandscript session as Get Booked differentiator
- **Why rejected:** Matt already does this for every client regardless of tier. It's table stakes, not an upgrade.

### Written content as Get Booked differentiator
- **Same reason:** Matt writes content for every tier. Not a differentiator.
