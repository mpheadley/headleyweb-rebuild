## Why This Matters If You're a Local Business Owner

You already know you need to show up on Google. You've probably claimed your Google Business Profile. Maybe you've asked a few customers for reviews.

But here's what's changing: **people aren't just Googling anymore.**

They're asking ChatGPT. They're asking Perplexity. They're asking Siri. And each AI tool has its own idea of who to recommend.

If your business only shows up in one place, you're invisible everywhere else.

## What Makes AI Recommend One Business Over Another?

It's not random. Each AI tool has patterns:

**ChatGPT** trusts review platforms. If you have strong reviews on Yelp, Angi, and BBB, ChatGPT is more likely to mention you. It also favors businesses with a clear, consistent description across the web — the same name, address, phone number, and description everywhere.

**Perplexity** trusts your website more than the others do. It reads your actual pages, not just your directory listings. If your site clearly says what you do, where you do it, and how to contact you — in plain language, not marketing fluff — Perplexity rewards that. It cited direct links to 14 local business websites in a single query.

**Google AI Overviews** pull from the content that already ranks — blog posts, YouTube videos, authoritative articles. If you've written helpful content that answers real questions, Google's AI can cite you.

## What You Can Do About It

You don't need to understand how AI works. You need to do five things:

1. **Make your website specific.** List every service. List every city you serve. Use plain language. Don't say "we provide comprehensive plumbing solutions" — say "we fix leaky pipes, install water heaters, and clear clogged drains in Anniston, Oxford, and Jacksonville, Alabama."

2. **Get reviews on more than one platform.** Google reviews help Google. But ChatGPT trusts Yelp and Angi. Gemini trusts Facebook. You need reviews in multiple places.

3. **Keep your business info consistent everywhere.** Same name, same address, same phone number — on your website, Google Business Profile, Yelp, Facebook, BBB, and every directory you're listed on. AI tools cross-reference these to decide if you're a real, trustworthy business.

4. **Add structured data to your website.** This is the behind-the-scenes code that tells AI exactly what your business is, where you're located, and what services you offer. Most template websites don't include it. Custom-built sites do.

5. **Answer the phone.** Seriously. AI is going to start making calls on behalf of customers. The first business that picks up wins the job. If you're sending leads to voicemail, no amount of SEO helps.
