# Template Sites vs. Custom-Built: What's the Real Difference?

**Angle:** Not a hit piece on templates. Some businesses genuinely should use Squarespace or a WordPress template. But for businesses that depend on their website for leads, the difference matters. Show the real tradeoffs without being preachy.

**Target reader:** Business owner who's been quoted $500 and $5,000 for "a website" and doesn't understand why.

---

## What a Template Site Actually Is

- Pre-built design you customize with your content
- Platforms: Squarespace, Wix, WordPress themes, GoDaddy builder
- Cost: $0-$500 (DIY) or $500-$1,500 (someone sets it up for you)
- Timeline: days to a couple weeks
- What you get: a website that exists

## What a Custom-Built Site Actually Is

- Designed and built specifically for your business goals
- Framework varies (WordPress custom theme, Next.js, etc.)
- Cost: $2,000-$10,000+ depending on scope
- Timeline: 2-6 weeks
- What you get: a website that works

## The Real Differences (Not What You Think)

### It's not about "looking custom"
- Modern templates look great. Nobody visits your site and thinks "ah, this is a template."
- The difference isn't visual — it's structural.

### Speed
- Templates carry code for features you don't use. A template site loads 50 things. Your visitor needs 5.
- Custom sites only load what's needed. Faster load = better Google ranking = more visibility.
- Show real PageSpeed comparison: template WordPress site vs. your Next.js builds.

### Copy and messaging
- Template sites have placeholder structure: hero image, "Welcome to [Business]," three feature boxes, contact form.
- That structure wasn't designed for YOUR customer's journey.
- Custom sites structure the page around what your customer needs to see, in the order they need to see it.
- (This is where StoryBrand thinking matters — don't use the word, describe the outcome.)

### SEO foundation
- Templates generate messy HTML, duplicate tags, bloated CSS.
- Search engines read code, not design. Clean code = easier to rank.
- Schema markup, proper heading hierarchy, semantic HTML — templates rarely get these right.

### Mobile experience
- Templates are "responsive" — they technically work on phones.
- Custom sites are "mobile-first" — they're designed for phones first, then expanded for desktop.
- 60-70% of local business website traffic is mobile. "Technically works" isn't enough.

## When a Template Is the Right Call

- You need a web presence but don't depend on it for leads (you get all your business from referrals)
- You're testing a business idea and need something up fast
- Your budget is genuinely under $1,000

## When Custom Is Worth the Investment

- Your website IS your lead generation (service businesses, contractors, professionals)
- You're competing locally and need to outrank other businesses
- You've had a template site and it's not getting calls
- You want a site that grows with your business, not one you'll replace in a year
