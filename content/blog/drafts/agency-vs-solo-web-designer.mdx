# Agency vs. Solo Web Designer: Which Is Right for Your Small Business?

**Angle:** Honest comparison. Don't trash agencies — some businesses genuinely need one. But make it clear that for most local service businesses, a focused solo designer who knows their market is the better fit. The reader should finish this and think "I don't need an agency."

**Target reader:** Local business owner comparing options. Maybe got quotes from both and doesn't know the difference.

---

## The Agency Model

- Team of specialists: designer, developer, copywriter, project manager, SEO person
- Higher overhead = higher prices (you're paying for the office, the team, the account manager)
- Your project gets divided across people who don't talk to each other
- You talk to a project manager, not the person building your site
- Good for: large companies with complex needs, e-commerce at scale, businesses with big budgets

## The Solo Designer Model

- One person handles strategy, design, development, copy, SEO
- Lower overhead = more competitive pricing
- You talk directly to the person doing the work — no telephone game
- Accountability is built in — there's no one else to blame
- Good for: local service businesses, small teams, businesses that want a relationship, not a vendor

## The Real Questions

### "Who's actually building my site?"
- At an agency, it might be a junior developer you never meet.
- With a solo designer, it's the person you shook hands with.

### "How fast can you move?"
- Agencies have queues, approval chains, and multiple clients ahead of you.
- A solo designer can start your project next week.

### "Will you understand my market?"
- A Birmingham or Atlanta agency might build a great-looking site, but do they know that half your customers come from the Anniston-Oxford corridor?
- A local designer knows the market because they live in it.

### "What happens when I need a quick change?"
- Agency: submit a ticket, wait for the project manager to assign it, get billed hourly.
- Solo designer: send a text, it's done by lunch.

## When an Agency IS the Right Call

Be honest here — credibility matters:
- You're an e-commerce brand doing $1M+ and need a custom platform
- You need a team of 5+ working on a rebrand simultaneously
- You have a dedicated marketing budget of $5K+/month for ongoing campaigns

## When a Solo Designer Is the Better Fit

- You're a local service business (plumber, HVAC, contractor, restaurant, etc.)
- Your budget is $2K-$8K for a website that actually works
- You want someone who'll pick up the phone
- You care more about getting calls than winning design awards
