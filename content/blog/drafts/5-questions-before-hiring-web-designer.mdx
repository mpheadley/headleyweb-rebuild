# 5 Questions to Ask Before You Hire a Web Designer in Northeast Alabama

**Angle:** Buyer's guide format. Each question is something the reader should ask ANY web designer — but the "right" answers naturally describe what you do and expose what template/agency shops don't. Never name competitors. Let the reader connect the dots.

**Target reader:** Local business owner who knows they need a website but doesn't know how to evaluate options. Maybe got a quote from someone, not sure if it's fair.

---

## 1. "Can I see who's actually building my site?"

- A lot of agencies hide behind a logo. No team page, no bios, no faces.
- You're trusting someone with your business's first impression — you should know who they are.
- Ask to see the person who'll do the work, not just the salesperson.
- **Your differentiator:** You show your face, your story, your process. One person, accountable.

## 2. "Will my site be fast on mobile, or just look good on your laptop?"

- Most small business websites are built on bloated templates. They look fine on a big screen but crawl on a phone.
- Google ranks mobile speed. Your customers are on their phones.
- Ask for a PageSpeed score. If they don't know what that is, that's your answer.
- **Your differentiator:** Next.js, optimized images, real performance scores. Don't say "Next.js" — say "my sites score 90+ on Google's speed test."

## 3. "Is the copy written for my customer, or for Google?"

- SEO matters. But if your homepage reads like a robot wrote it, visitors bounce.
- Good copy speaks to the customer's problem first, then works in the keywords naturally.
- Ask: "Who writes the copy?" If the answer is "we generate it" or "we pull from a template," that's a red flag.
- **Your differentiator:** StoryBrand framework. Don't use the word "StoryBrand" here — describe it: "I write your site so the customer sees their problem and your solution in the first 5 seconds."

## 4. "What happens after launch?"

- A website isn't a one-time project. It needs updates, security patches, content changes, performance monitoring.
- Ask what ongoing support looks like. Is there a plan? What does it cost? What's included?
- If the answer is "call us if something breaks," that's not a plan.
- **Your differentiator:** Care Plans with defined scope, monthly reporting, predictable pricing.

## 5. "Am I paying for AI tools or for someone who understands my business?"

- AI is everywhere now. Every agency claims they "use AI." That's fine — AI is a tool.
- The question is: does someone who understands YOUR market, YOUR customers, and YOUR goals decide what goes on the page?
- AI can write a meta description. It can't sit across from you at a coffee shop and figure out why your current site isn't getting calls.
- **Your differentiator:** You use AI as a tool (be honest about it), but the strategy is human. The discovery call, the messaging framework, the local market knowledge — that's you.
