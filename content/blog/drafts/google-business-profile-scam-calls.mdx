## OUTLINE

### Hook (2-3 sentences)
You're in the middle of a job and your phone rings. "This is Google calling about your business listing." Sounds important. It's not.

### The Flood Is Real (stats section)
- Frame the scale: 35% of owners get these calls weekly, 78% are AI robocalls
- Only 3% of calls claiming to be from Google are actually from Google
- As of early 2026, 40% of GBP "leads" are spam
- This isn't a minor annoyance — it's an industry

### What the Scammers Say (and why it works)
- "Your listing is about to be removed" — creates panic
- "You need to pay to keep your listing active" — GBP is free, always has been
- "We can get you to the top of Google" — vague, unprovable
- "Press 1 to speak with a representative" — classic robocall escalation
- Fake review extortion: blast you with 1-stars, then offer to remove them for a fee
- Why it works: business owners don't know what Google actually does and doesn't do

### What Google Will Never Do
Clear, scannable list:
- Never charges for your Google Business Profile (it's free)
- Never cold-calls to sell advertising or SEO
- Never threatens to remove your listing as a first contact
- Never asks for your password or payment over the phone
- Legit Google calls come from 650 area code, and reps have @xwf.google.com emails
- Google may call to verify info (hours, services) but never to sell

### What to Do When You Get the Call
1. Hang up. Don't press any numbers.
2. Don't give out login info, payment info, or your Google account password
3. Block the number
4. Report to FTC (reportfraud.ftc.gov) and Google
5. If you're worried, go directly to business.google.com and check your profile yourself

### How to Actually Protect Your Listing
- Claim and verify your profile if you haven't (link to google-business-profile-setup post)
- Enable 2-factor authentication on your Google account
- Set up Google alerts for your business name
- Check your profile monthly — look for edits you didn't make (Google lets anyone "suggest" changes)
- Don't hand your login to anyone who cold-called you

### The Real Problem Under the Scam
- These calls work because most business owners aren't sure their profile IS set up right
- They haven't looked at it in months (or ever)
- The anxiety the scammers exploit is real — "am I showing up? Is my info correct?"
- The fix isn't paying a stranger on the phone. It's spending 30 minutes on your actual profile.
