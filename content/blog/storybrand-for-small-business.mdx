Pull up your website right now. Read the first sentence on the homepage. Is it about you, or about the person you're trying to help?

If it starts with "Welcome to" or "We are" or "Since 1987" — your site is talking about itself. And your visitors are leaving.

StoryBrand fixes that.

## What Is StoryBrand?

StoryBrand is a messaging framework created by Donald Miller. The core idea is simple: **people don't buy the best products — they buy the ones they understand the fastest.**

Your website has about 5 seconds to answer three questions before someone hits the back button:

1. What do you offer?
2. How does it make my life better?
3. What do I do next?

If your homepage doesn't answer all three above the fold, you're losing people. StoryBrand gives you a structure to answer them clearly every time.

## The 7-Part Framework (Without the Jargon)

StoryBrand follows the same narrative arc as every good movie. Here's how it maps to your business:

### 1. A Character (Your Customer)

The hero of your website is **not your business**. It's your customer. They're the one with the problem, and your site exists to show them you understand it.

A plumber's hero section shouldn't say "We've been in business since 2001." It should say "**Your pipes are leaking and you need it fixed today.**"

### 2. Has a Problem

Every customer has three levels of problems:

- **External:** The tangible thing — "My AC isn't working"
- **Internal:** How it makes them feel — "I'm frustrated and overwhelmed"
- **Philosophical:** Why it's not fair — "A hardworking person shouldn't have to suffer through an Alabama summer without AC"

Most websites only address the external problem. But people make decisions based on how they **feel**. Address all three and your copy gets a lot more powerful.

### 3. Meets a Guide (That's You)

Here's where most businesses get it wrong — they position themselves as the hero. But your customer is already the hero of their own story. What they need is a **guide**.

A good guide shows two things:

- **Empathy:** "I know how frustrating it is when your website isn't generating leads."
- **Authority:** "I've built sites for dozens of local businesses that actually get found on Google."

You need both.

### 4. Who Gives Them a Plan

People don't take action when they're confused. They need a simple, clear path forward. Three steps is the sweet spot:

1. Request your free checkup
2. I build your site
3. Your phone starts ringing

That's it. No ten-step onboarding process. No "comprehensive solutions." Three steps that make the next move obvious.

### 5. And Calls Them to Action

Your website needs a **direct call to action** — a button that says exactly what to do. "Get Started," "Call Now," "Book a Free Consultation."

Not "Learn More." Not "Explore Our Services." Those are soft asks that let people procrastinate. A direct CTA tells them: this is the next step. Take it.

Put it in the hero. Put it in the navigation. Put it at the end of every section. You can't overdo it.

### 6. That Helps Them Avoid Failure

What happens if your customer does nothing? This is the part most small business websites skip — and it's one of the most important.

For a contractor: "Every month without a working website, your competitors are getting the calls that should be going to you."

You're not being manipulative. You're being honest about what's at stake. If the stakes are real, say so.

### 7. And Ends in Success

Paint the picture. What does life look like after they work with you?

- "Your phone rings with qualified leads instead of spam"
- "You show up first when someone searches your trade in your city"
- "You stop worrying about where your next customer is coming from"

This is what they're really buying — not a website, not a service. They're buying the **result**.

## Why This Works for Small Businesses

Big companies can afford to be vague. They have brand recognition and marketing budgets. You don't.

As a small business, every visitor counts. StoryBrand works because it strips away the noise and gives people exactly what they need to make a decision:

- **Clarity** over cleverness
- **Their problem** before your credentials
- **A simple path** instead of a maze of pages

I've seen business owners rewrite a single hero section using this framework and start getting more calls the same week. Not because the design changed — because the **message** finally landed.

## How to Apply StoryBrand to Your Website Today

You don't need to hire anyone to start. Open your homepage and ask yourself:

1. **Does the hero talk about my customer's problem?** If it starts with "Welcome to..." or "About us...", rewrite it.
2. **Is there a clear call to action above the fold?** If someone has to scroll to find out what to do next, add a button.
3. **Can a stranger tell what I do in 5 seconds?** Show your homepage to someone who doesn't know your business. If they can't answer "what do you do and why should I care?" — your messaging needs work.
4. **Am I showing the stakes?** If there's no consequence for inaction, there's no urgency to act.
5. **Am I painting the success?** If you never show what life looks like after, you're selling a process instead of a result.

## The Short Version

Your customer has a problem. You can solve it. StoryBrand helps you say it in a way they actually hear.
