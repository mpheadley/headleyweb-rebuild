### GBP-First Sales Bridge

Google Business Profile is the real front door for local service businesses —
often matters more than the website itself.

**"Foot in the Door" Strategy:**
1. Offer **GBP Optimization + 1-Page Site** at a low entry price ($495 Get Found)
2. Collect performance data (calls, impressions, map pack ranking)
3. Show the client: "My work increased your calls by X%"
4. Price sensitivity evaporates — upsell to Get Calls / Get Booked / Sales Engine

**The math to $400K:** You don't need 300 local shops. You need **10 high-value
partnerships**. Once recurring revenue from $49–$149/mo plans covers living expenses,
you gain the financial margin to stop taking low-budget work and pitch larger
regional companies using the case studies you built locally.

## Launch Strategy: First 3 Clients Free

> **Pre-launch:** Before announcing the business publicly, follow the personal social re-entry sequence in Claude memory (`social-launch-strategy.md`). That plan builds visible credibility through shipped projects before this phase begins.

**Goal:** Build reps and social proof fast. First 3 client builds are free — no build fee, no monthly charges for 6 months.

**What to get in writing (before starting):**
- [ ] Google review within 1 week of launch
- [ ] Testimonial quote (with name + photo permission) for headleyweb.com
- [ ] Social media post tagging Headley Web & SEO
- [ ] Permission to use site as a before/after case study
- [ ] Agreement to be a verbal reference for future leads

**On the proposal:** Show the full retail price crossed out (e.g. ~~$795 build + $149/mo~~). The client sees the value; you set the anchor for paid clients.

**Scope cap:** Starter tier max (5 pages). No scope creep into Professional/Sales Engine territory.

**After 6 months:** Natural conversion point — they're already in the habit and seeing value. Pitch Essential Care ($49/mo) or Accelerate ($149/mo).

### Case Study Build Pitch (verbal framing)

> **Pre-meeting research:** Before any discovery call, run the 8-step process in [DISCOVERY_WORKFLOW.md](DISCOVERY_WORKFLOW.md). It takes 30–60 minutes and gives you the audit numbers, competitor names, and GBP status you need to make the pitch concrete. The baseline metrics you capture also become the "before" numbers for the case study.

Use this when presenting a free build to a prospect. Anchor value first, then reveal the arrangement.

> "A site like this typically runs **$795 to $1,195**. I'm building yours free as part of a case study — I document the before/after and use it on my site. After 3 months, the site is yours. At that point you can take everything and host it yourself, or stay on an optional care plan: **$49/mo** covers updates and hosting, **$149/mo** adds Google Business Profile management and ongoing SEO."

**Why this order matters:** Lead with the retail value so the prospect understands what they're getting. If you lead with "it's free," they anchor to zero and the care plan feels like a bait-and-switch. If you lead with $795–$1,195 and *then* say it's free, the care plan feels cheap by comparison.

**Care plan framing after anchor:**
- $49/mo = updates + hosting. "Basically just keeping the lights on."
- $149/mo = adds GBP management + ongoing SEO. "This is where you actually keep moving up in search."

### Nonprofit / Community Builds (Portfolio + Goodwill)

Nonprofit and community organization builds are high-value portfolio pieces even at zero revenue. They generate emotionally compelling case studies, community goodwill, and network effects (churches talk to churches, outreach centers talk to outreach centers).

**Pipeline:**
- [ ] **Jacksonville Christian Outreach Center** — Jacob (friend) built their current site ~4 years ago for free, says they want to update it summer 2026 and is happy to hand it off. Clean handoff — confirm Jacob is fully passing it off, not expecting to stay involved. Get signed agreement (testimonial, Google review, case study permission). Screenshot the current site as "before" baseline. Strong Local Legends crossover potential.

**Why nonprofits work for Phase 1:**
- Zero sales friction — they need help and can't pay agencies
- Emotionally compelling before/after (people share nonprofit transformations)
- Community positioning — "the guy who helps local organizations" not "the guy trying to sell me something"
- Network effect — one nonprofit build leads to referrals within the same community (churches, shelters, food banks)
- Pairs with Local Legends profiles

### Gap Action Plans

**1. Video / YouTube Strategy (Authority Content)**
- **What:** Record website checkups as YouTube content. Same skill as the site checkup lead magnet, but public.
- **Format:** "I Review a Real [Trade] Website" — 5-10 min screen recordings with StoryBrand commentary
- **Why it works:** Demonstrates expertise, ranks for "[trade] website" keywords, feeds into site checkup CTA
- **When:** Phase 2 (month 2-4). Start with 1 video/month alongside blog posts.
- **Low-effort start:** Record the free site checkups you're already doing (with client permission), edit lightly, post to YouTube.

**2. Year-Long Nurture Sequence (Smart Nurture Engine)**
- **What:** After the 5-email post-checkup sequence, prospects enter a long-tail weekly/biweekly email.
- **Content:** Blog post highlights, quick tips, local business spotlights, case study snippets, seasonal reminders ("Q4 is when people search for holiday services — is your site ready?")
- **Goal:** Stay top-of-mind for 12 months. Most local business owners take 3-6 months to decide.
- **When:** Phase 2. Draft 12-24 evergreen emails (Claude-drafted, you edit). Set up in email platform.
- **Tool:** ConvertKit, Mailchimp, or MailerLite — whichever you choose for the lead generator.

**3. Evergreen Webinar (Highest-Leverage Lead Magnet)**
- **What:** A recorded 30-45 min presentation: "How the StoryBrand Framework Gets Your Phone Ringing"
- **Structure:** Problem (your website talks about you, not your customer) → Framework (7 elements, simplified) → Examples (before/after of real sites) → CTA (site checkup or strategy call)
- **Why:** Positions you as the authority. Prospects self-select — anyone who watches 30 min is warm.
- **When:** Phase 3 (month 3-6). Requires 2-3 case studies for the "examples" section.
- **Distribution:** Blog post embed, email sequence link, LinkedIn, YouTube, services page.

**4. Pitch Deck (Sales Call Structure)**
- **What:** A StoryBrand-formatted slide deck for strategy calls. Not a generic agency deck.
- **Structure (10-12 slides):**
  1. Their problem (what you found in the checkup)
  2. What's at stake (cost of inaction — BrandScript #6)
  3. The plan (3 steps — BrandScript #4)
  4. What success looks like (BrandScript #7 — before/after)
  5. Social proof (case study metrics, testimonial)
  6. Pricing options (build tiers + care plans)
  7. CTA (next step — "pick a tier, I'll send the invoice")
- **When:** Phase 2. Build before the first paid sales call.
- **Tool:** Google Slides or Keynote. Keep it simple — no animations, no 50-slide decks.

**5. AI Visibility Audit (Two-Phase Lead Magnet)**
- **Phase A — Manual (launch immediately):** When a lead submits the audit form, search their business name in ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity. Screen-record a 2-minute Loom showing what AI says (or doesn't say) about them. Include this as part of the free site checkup — one lead magnet, two hooks ("Get Your Free Site Checkup" + "Want to see what AI says about your business?").
- **Why manual first:** Takes 5 minutes per lead, feels incredibly personal, proves your expertise before they ever talk to you. No one else in NE Alabama is offering this.
- **Phase B — Automated (Phase 3, month 3-6):** Build AEO Brand Grader MVP — prospect enters business name, tool queries AI APIs and returns a report. Captures the lead and auto-generates a score. Reduces per-lead time from 5 min to near-zero.
- **Upsell path:** AI visibility check → free site checkup → Accelerate care plan ($149/mo includes AI visibility monitoring, quarterly entity audit, answer-first content refresh).
- **Website integration:** AI callout bar on homepage after Pain Points section. "Want to see what AI says about your business?" → links to the checkup form. Same form, different emotional hook.

**6. Launch Day Push (Client Deliverable)**
- **What:** A 15-minute checklist handed to the client on launch day. Three actions: share on social media, ask 2-3 past customers for Google reviews, update links everywhere (GBP, Facebook, Instagram, email signature).
- **Why it works:** Google sees a spike in traffic + new reviews in the first week and accelerates indexing and local ranking. Also builds the client's ownership of their online presence.
- **Template:** `~/Documents/Web Development/starter-kit/LAUNCH_DAY_PUSH.md` — fill in client name, domain, date, review link.
- **When:** Every launch, starting Phase 1. Zero cost, high impact.

**9. Case Study Pipeline (Proof Engine)**
- **What:** A structured process to turn every client project into a metrics-first case study that justifies higher pricing.
- **Pre-meeting research:** Use [DISCOVERY_WORKFLOW.md](DISCOVERY_WORKFLOW.md) to capture baseline metrics, competitor gaps, and GBP status before the discovery call. These become your "before" numbers automatically.
- **Baseline "before" snapshot:** On day 1, save Lighthouse scores + full-page screenshots of the old site (or document "no site"). Store in `before-screenshots/` in the project folder. Added to Launch Checklist Phase 0.
- **AI Share of Voice (SOV) tracking:** At launch and at 30/60/90 days, query ChatGPT and Perplexity for the client's services + city. Screenshot results. Track whether the client appears as a recommendation. This is raw proof for the Accelerate care plan and AEO positioning.
- **Signed agreement as commitment filter:** Free builds require a signed agreement: testimonial upon completion + care plan commitment after the 3-month grace period. Filters out tire-kickers and guarantees you get the testimonial and recurring revenue. Added to Launch Checklist Phase 0.
- **Domain ownership policy:** You register and manage the domain (cost included in service — ~$12/year). Transfer to client if they leave (unlock + auth code, 5 minutes). Zero friction for new clients, honors the "you own everything" promise. `DOMAIN_STRATEGY.md` covers the domain selection decision.
- **Metrics-first case study template:**
  1. Big headline number (e.g., "300% increase in mobile leads in 90 days")
  2. The "before" — Lighthouse scores, old site screenshots, "invisible in map pack"
  3. What we built — StoryBrand messaging, schema stack, GBP setup
  4. The "after" — GA4 traffic/calls/conversions, map pack position, AI SOV screenshots
  5. Client testimonial (collected via the 60-day automated email from Gap #8)
  6. CTA: "Want results like these? Get Your Free Site Checkup"
- **When:** Phase 1. The baseline snapshot and agreement happen before the build. SOV tracking starts at launch. Case study published at 90-day milestone.

**10. Quarterly Performance Review (Own Site + Client Sites)**
- **What:** A recurring audit cycle to track site health, catch regressions, and generate proof for case studies and retainer value.
- **Headleyweb.com (your own site):**
  - Run Lighthouse (mobile + desktop) quarterly. Save reports to `headleyweb-rebuild/lighthouse-baselines/`.
  - Review Google Search Console: impressions, clicks, average position, top queries. Screenshot the Performance tab.
  - Use scores to populate the `<PageSpeedProof />` widget on headleyweb.com — your own site is your best demo.
- **Client sites (every active retainer client):**
  - At 30/60/90 days post-launch, pull GSC data: impressions, clicks, average position, top queries, indexed pages.
  - Run Lighthouse at 30/90 days — compare to launch baseline in `before-screenshots/`.
  - Check AI SOV (from Gap #9) at same intervals.
  - Package the 90-day data into the case study (Gap #9 template).
  - Share a simple traffic report with the client at 30 days (builds trust, justifies retainer).
- **Tools:** Lighthouse CLI (`npx lighthouse`), Google Search Console (browser), PageSpeed Insights (for Next.js sites where CLI fails).
- **When:** Ongoing. First run at launch, then 30/60/90 days, then quarterly for retainer clients. Own site quarterly on the 1st of Jan/Apr/Jul/Oct.

**12. Interactive Quiz Lead Magnet**
- **What:** An engaging, personality-style quiz on headleyweb.com that business owners actually want to take — then captures their email for the result.
- **Why it works:** Quizzes have 2-4x higher conversion rates than static lead magnets. They feel fun, not salesy. The result gives you a natural follow-up ("Your site scored X — here's what to fix").
- **Format options (pick one to start):**
  - **"What's Your Business's Online Personality?"** — 8-10 questions about how they market, what they post, how they get leads. Result: one of 5 archetypes (The Ghost, The Billboard, The Word-of-Mouth King, The Social Butterfly, The Sales Machine). Each archetype gets a custom breakdown of what's working, what's missing, and the #1 thing to fix. Shareable result graphic.
  - **"The $10K Question: Is Your Website Making or Losing You Money?"** — Frame each question as a dollar amount. "Your homepage loads in 8 seconds — that's costing you $X/month in lost leads." Final score = estimated annual revenue lost. Gut-punch numbers motivate action.
  - **"Build Your Dream Website in 60 Seconds"** — A visual quiz where they pick hero layouts, color palettes, and section styles. At the end: "Here's what your site could look like" + a mockup preview. Captures email for "full design brief." Fun, aspirational, zero friction.
  - **"How Does Google See Your Business?"** — Questions about their GBP, schema, reviews, site speed. Result: a letter grade (A-F) with specific fixes. Pairs perfectly with the free video audit offer.
- **Tech:** Build as a React component on headleyweb.com. Email capture via Formspree or ConvertKit. Shareable result page with OG image for social sharing.
- **When:** Phase 2. Build after headleyweb.com launches. One quiz to start, test conversion, then iterate.

### The Two Battlefields (March 2026 — validated by live testing)

Local search now has two distinct battlefields. Understanding which one you're fighting on changes what you build and how you sell it.

| Battlefield | Query Type | What Google Shows | AI Overview? | Your Play |
|------------|-----------|-------------------|-------------|-----------|
| **Map Pack** | "plumber near me", "hvac near me" | Map pack (3-8 businesses), sponsored first | No — only in "People Also Ask" | GBP optimization is #1. Website supports the map listing. |
| **AI Answers** | "house isn't cooling", "pipes leaking under sink" | AI Overview with citations (articles, not local biz), then GBP below, then Reddit/YouTube | Yes | Answer-first blog content + YouTube that AI can cite |

**The indirect path from AI to local leads:**
Problem search ("house isn't cooling") → AI Overview cites an article/video → user reads answer, realizes they need a pro → searches "hvac near me" → map pack decides → your client gets the call.

**Implications for your services:**
- **Every build:** GBP optimization is still the #1 priority. Map pack = front door for local intent.
- **Blog/YouTube strategy:** More important than previously estimated. Informational queries trigger AI Overviews that cite blog posts, YouTube, and Reddit. Your blog roundups and YouTube checkup videos are the path into AI citations.
- **Backlinks:** Traditional backlinks matter less. Being cited by sources AI trusts (YouTube, Reddit threads, authoritative blog posts) matters more. YouTube checkup videos and blog roundup posts = citation seeds.
- **Sales pitch nuance:** "When someone searches 'plumber near me,' the map pack decides. When someone searches 'my pipes are leaking,' AI decides. I make sure you show up in both."
- **Don't overstate AI in customer-facing copy.** Most clients feel the map pack problem, not the AI problem. Lead with what they already feel (invisible on Google), then layer in the AI shift as the thing that makes it worse.
