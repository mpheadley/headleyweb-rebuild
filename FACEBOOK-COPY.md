Southern Legends is a storytelling project about the people and places in Northeast Alabama that most people drive past without knowing what they're missing.

Most people in Gadsden have been to Noccalula Falls a hundred times. But there are Civil War-era carvings in the gorge walls that almost nobody notices. I wrote about the park, what it meant to the Cherokee, and why it still matters.

### Anniston Museums & Gardens (for Anniston / Calhoun County groups)
There are two world-class museums and 15 acres of botanical gardens sitting on a ridge above Highway 431 in Anniston. Most people in Alabama have no idea they're there. I wrote about what's inside.

[link]

### Freedom Riders National Monument (for Anniston / Calhoun County groups)
On Mother's Day 1961, a Greyhound bus carrying Freedom Riders was firebombed outside Anniston. The national monument preserving the two attack sites was designated in 2017. My kids were born at the same hospital that treated the injured riders. I wrote about what happened and what the city is still reckoning with.

[link]

### Interfaith Ministries (for Anniston / Calhoun County groups)
Since 1975, a coalition of churches in Calhoun County has been pooling resources to help people in crisis. Free dental care, Meals on Wheels, emergency rent assistance. They run almost entirely on volunteers. I wrote about how it started and what it looks like now.

[link]
