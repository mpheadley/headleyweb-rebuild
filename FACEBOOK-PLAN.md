## 2. Southern Legends — Page Setup

### Profile
- **Page name:** Southern Legends
- **Category:** Media/News Company (primary), Community, Magazine
- **Bio:** "Stories about the people and places that make Northeast Alabama's Appalachian foothills worth knowing."
- **Profile photo:** "SL" typographic mark in Fraunces on forest green, or OG image cropped square
- **Cover photo:** Full OG image (forest green→gold gradient + topo + Fraunces)
- **CTA button:** "Learn More" → southernlegends.blog (once deployed)

### Content (post with every new profile)
- 2-3 sentence hook per profile, not just a link
  - Example: "Most people in Anniston have no idea what's inside the museum on Highway 431. I wrote about it."
- Share to community groups with custom intros (not copy-paste)
- Tag the subject's page if they have one
- Nostalgia hooks — "Did you grow up swimming below Noccalula Falls?"
- Milestone posts: at 5 profiles share the site itself, at 10 pitch local media

### Per-Post Checklist
1. **Upload multiple native photos** — faces outperform landscapes. Lead with a photo of you/Heather/family at the location (upload it first — Facebook makes the first image largest in the grid). 3-4 photos is the sweet spot. Facebook's algorithm favors native photo uploads over link preview cards.
2. **Drop the bare link in the first comment** (as the Page, no accompanying text), not the post body. The photo gets the reach, the link is right there for anyone who wants it.
3. **Check in** at the location (adds local visibility, shows up in that location's feed)
4. **Tag the subject's Facebook page** in the post (Pages can tag Pages)
5. **Share to your personal profile** after publishing from the SL page — tag Heather and anyone else in the photo from your personal share. Pages can only tag other Pages, not personal profiles, so the personal reshare is how you get personal tags and reach friends' feeds.
6. **Skip the Reels upsell** — Facebook prompts it on every photo post. Dismiss it.
7. **Wait to invite friends** until the page has 3-4 posts. An empty page with followers looks worse than a small page with content.

### Groups to Target
- Calhoun County community groups
- NE Alabama groups
- Jacksonville groups
- JSU alumni groups
- Local buy/sell/trade (sparingly, when relevant)

## 3. Relationship Between the Two Pages

- **Southern Legends builds trust and visibility** — community-facing, editorial, no CTAs
- **Headley Web converts trust into business** — professional, has CTAs, links to services
- Cross-post SL profiles from Headley Web occasionally, but don't mirror
- **Only cross-share profiles that connect to web design** — business owners, nonprofits with a "this is why I build for local businesses" angle. Don't cross-share personal/place posts (Noccalula, Cheaha, covered bridges) — they don't tie to HW's mission.
- Keep editorial and business lines separate (SL = narrative, HW = business)
