### StoryBrand Homepage Section Flow
Every strong SB site follows this order:
1. **Hero** — headline addresses customer's problem (not your business name)
2. **Stakes** — "What happens if you do nothing?"
3. **Problem** — 3 pain points with icons
4. **Guide** — empathy + authority (photo + "I know what it's like...")
5. **Plan** — 3 steps (Consult → Build → Launch)
6. **CTA** — prominent, repeated throughout
7. **Success** — testimonials + results

### Key Patterns
- Trade-specific landing pages ("/web-design-for-plumbers") are standard for SEO
- Subscription models ($150-$200/mo) are becoming common alongside project-based pricing
- "We understand your industry" messaging is the #1 differentiator
- Before/after project galleries are common for trade clients
- Several bundle SEO + Google Ads with web design

### Actionable Ideas
1. **Trade-specific landing pages** — combine with location pages for SEO matrix (e.g. "/web-design-for-plumbers-anniston-al")

3. **Career transition story** — "Pastor → web designer" = trust signal in Bible Belt (same playbook as Strictly Plumbers)
4. **PageSpeed screenshots as social proof** — Oak Harbor shows Lighthouse scores, proves technical quality

### Metrics-First Case Study Format (Studio Ubique)
1. **Big headline number** ("4,296% traffic growth in 6 months")
2. Project name
3. 15-word description
4. Device mockup screenshot
5. Service tags (SEO, Design, etc.)
6. "View Project" link for full details

### The Gap We Can Exploit
Almost no freelancer serving local/trade businesses publishes hard metrics. Most show screenshots + testimonials but skip the numbers. If we track GA4 data on client sites and publish real results (traffic increase, phone calls, form submissions), we'll be in the top 1% of portfolio credibility for this market.

### Action Items for Portfolio Page
- [ ] Lead with the metric, not the screenshot
- [ ] Use the case study template from PLAN.md
- [ ] Track GA4 events on all client sites from day one
- [ ] Screenshot client sites before launch (the "before")
- [ ] Check metrics at 30 and 60 days post-launch
- [ ] Frame redesigns as ROI/investment, not creative service
