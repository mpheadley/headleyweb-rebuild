## 1. Character (The Customer)

**Who they are:**
Local service business owners in Northeast Alabama: plumbers, HVAC techs, contractors, woodworkers, restaurants, nonprofits. 1-10 employees. Technically proficient in their trade but short on time and digital marketing knowledge.

**What they want:**
A professional, lead-generating online presence that makes their phone ring without requiring them to manage a complex marketing stack.

---

## 2. Problem

**External problem** (the tangible thing that's broken):
Their business is invisible in the Google map pack, the #1 way local customers choose who to call. And the landscape is shifting: for problem-aware searches ("house isn't cooling," "pipes leaking"), AI now answers the question before the searcher ever sees a local business. There are now two places you need to show up, and most local businesses aren't set up for either one.

> *Nuance: "plumber near me" queries still go straight to the map pack (no AI Overview). AI Overviews trigger on informational/problem-aware queries and cite articles, YouTube, and Reddit, not local businesses directly. The path from AI to local leads is indirect: problem search → AI cites content → user realizes they need a pro → "near me" search → map pack. GBP optimization remains priority #1; answer-first content is the path into AI citations.*
>
> *Stat source: 83% zero-click rate when AI Overviews trigger (click-vision.com, cited in AEO_RESEARCH.md). Use "over 80%" in customer-facing copy for simplicity; cite 83% in blog posts and data-driven contexts.*

**Internal problem** (how it makes them feel):
Frustration and confusion. Frustrated that the rules keep changing, embarrassed when someone asks for their website, confused by "SEO" and "AI visibility" jargon, and anxious that they're falling behind in ways they can't even see.

**Philosophical problem** (why it's just wrong):
A hardworking Alabama business owner shouldn't be paying for something that works against them. Your success should be determined by the quality of your work — not by whether someone sold you the right website.

---

## 3. Guide (The Business)

**Empathy statement** (prove you understand their frustration):
As a husband, dad, and former pastor living right here in Jacksonville, I know that running a small business isn't just about "marketing." It's about providing for your family and serving your community. I got tired of watching good businesses send money to agencies that don't know their town, don't know their customers, and disappear after the check clears.

**Authority markers** (why they should trust you):
- Show Up on Google Maps: GBP optimization so you land in the top 3 on Maps
- You Own Everything: no contracts, no hostage situations. Site, domain, and data from day one. Optional care plans, not traps.
- Fast on Every Phone: loads fast, easy to call or book in one tap
- Built for Your Market: every page targets local search terms, Jacksonville to Gadsden and everywhere in between
- StoryBrand messaging framework (customer-first, not agency-first)
- AI Visibility: I make sure Google AND AI recommend your business, not just index it
- Reviews Feed AI: as of 2025, GBP reviews directly influence what AI search tools (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews) recommend. More recent reviews = stronger AI citation signal. This makes review coaching (Growth Care plan) a technical SEO necessity, not a soft perk.

## 6. Failure (Stakes)

**What happens if they DON'T act:**

- You keep losing high-value leads to competitors who adapted to the new search landscape, even if their work isn't as good as yours
- Your website stays invisible in the map pack and absent from AI answers, and every month of inaction is another month of lost revenue you can't get back
- The gap widens: AI is already picking favorites, and the businesses it recommends today get stronger tomorrow. The longer you wait, the harder it gets to catch up.

---

## 7. Success (The Transformation)

**What life looks like AFTER:**
A consistent flow of local calls and jobs from a search-optimized presence that works across Google, AI answers, and the map pack. The owner moves from confusion and embarrassment to confidence and pride, knowing their business shows up wherever customers are searching, stable instead of anxious.

**Before → After:**

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| Invisible contractor losing leads to the new search landscape | Respected local authority found at the top of Google and recommended by AI |
| Overwhelmed and confused by changing technology | Confident and proud when sharing their website |
| Anxiety-filled reliance on sporadic referrals | Stability and peace from a consistent lead machine that adapts as search evolves |

## Lead Generator (Two-Step Funnel)

### Step 1: Automated Site Checkup (lead capture)

**Format:** Instant branded PDF report, emailed via Resend

**CTA on site:** "Get Your Free Site Checkup" (primary) / "Take the Quiz" (secondary)

**What it delivers:** A multi-page PDF with overall letter grade, mobile screenshot, messaging analysis (16 scored items), Lighthouse scores, Core Web Vitals, SEO/accessibility checks, and "What I'd Fix First" recommendations.

**What it captures:** Email address + website URL (via audit form) or email + quiz answers (via quiz)

**Where it lives on the site:** Hero section (primary CTA button), services page, blog post CTAs, nav link ("Free Audit")

### Step 2: Free Video Review (manual follow-up, trust-builder)

**Format:** Personalized 3-5 minute Loom screen recording

**What it delivers:** Matt walks through their specific site, referencing the PDF scores: mobile usability, missing CTAs, GBP gaps, page speed, messaging clarity. Personal and specific, not automated.

**When it happens:** Offered as a free follow-up to every audit lead. Email or call within 24-48 hours of receiving the PDF.

**Why 2 steps:** The PDF scales: it captures the lead without Matt's time. The video converts: it builds trust and demonstrates expertise before asking for the sale. The PDF gets forwarded to partners/spouses; the video builds the relationship with the decision-maker.

**Follow-up:** See Nurture Email Sequence below.

## Nurture Email Sequence (5 emails after lead generator, then year-long nurture)

> These 5 emails run immediately after the site checkup. After Day 21,
> prospects enter a long-tail biweekly sequence (12-24 evergreen emails)
> to stay top-of-mind for up to 12 months. See BUSINESS_STRATEGY.md
> Gap Action Plan #2 for the year-long nurture plan.

| # | Send Day | Subject Line | BrandScript Element | Goal |
|---|----------|-------------|---------------------|------|
| 1 | Day 0 | "Here's your free site checkup" | **Guide**, empathy | Deliver the audit, establish trust |
| 2 | Day 3 | "Your competitors are easier to find (here's why)" | **Problem**, external + internal | Agitate the visibility pain |
| 3 | Day 7 | "3 steps to a website that actually generates leads" | **Plan**, 3 steps | Show how easy it is to work with you |
| 4 | Day 14 | "How [CLIENT] went from invisible to booked solid" | **Success**, transformation | Prove it works with a real example |
| 5 | Day 21 | "Every month you wait is another month of lost leads" | **Call to Action** + **Failure** | Ask for the sale, show cost of inaction |

**Email 1, Deliver + Empathize:**
*(Subject: "Here's your free site checkup")*
PDF report is attached automatically by the audit tool. Acknowledge their situation ("I know you're busy running your business, and the last thing you need is another tech headache"). Offer the free video walkthrough: "I'd be happy to record a quick video walking through your results, no cost, no pitch. Just reply and I'll send it over." Preview what's coming in the next few emails.

**Email 2, Name the Problem:**
*(Subject: "Your competitors are easier to find (here's why)")*
Describe what happens when someone searches "[their trade] near me." The business with the better website and Google profile wins, even if their work isn't as good. Name the frustration of losing jobs to less-skilled competitors.

**Email 3, Show the Plan:**
*(Subject: "3 steps to a website that actually generates leads")*
Lay out the 3-step plan. Emphasize: you handle everything, flat rate, no jargon. Make it feel simple and low-risk.

**Email 4, Social Proof:**
*(Subject: "How [CLIENT] went from invisible to booked solid")*
Tell a Before → After story from a real client. Include the metric (calls increased by X%, moved from page 3 to map pack).

**Email 5, Direct CTA:**
*(Subject: "Every month you wait is another month of lost leads")*
Name the stakes (competitors keep getting stronger, your website keeps aging). Paint the success picture. Make the direct CTA: "Schedule a 15-Minute Call." Include phone number.
